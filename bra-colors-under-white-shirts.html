Color Guide

Under white shirts, color is only half the answer.

A shade close to your skin tone often matters, but cup texture matters too. Fabric cups can be easier under light shirts than lace cups with visible texture.

Direct answer

Choose low contrast and low texture.

Many people assume a white bra is best under a white shirt. In practice, bright white can show because it contrasts with skin. A low-contrast shade is often easier. Then choose a fabric cup when the shirt is thin or fitted.

Better under white

Low-contrast colors and smoother fabric cups.

More visible

High-contrast black, bright white, or textured lace under thin shirts.

Best for / not best for

Best for: everyday outfits, work tees, lighter tops and fuller-figure shoppers who want a simple weekly color rotation.

Not best for: outfits that intentionally show a bra as styling, where darker colors may be the goal.
